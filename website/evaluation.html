The Initial Plan for Term 2


So far we have managed to gather our MoSCoW requirements in the form of Functionality and Usability requirements from our client. Following that, we used User Interface Design principles and Human Computer Interaction concepts to design an effective UI which the client gave positive feedback on.

After which we decided on the Frontend and Backend frameworks that we will use to develop the mobile app through detailed research and proceeded to perform experiments with them. Lastly, we developed a Prototype which we demonstrated to our client and received their approval to go further with development.

Our main plan for the next term involves starting development with Firebase and Ionic to produce the Electronic Care Plan. We aim to complete all the Must and Should Have Requirements to satisfy the client. We will use Trello to facilitate the Agile Scrum Development Methodology which will aid in project management and collaborative processes.

We will utilise two week sprints so that we consistently receive feedback about the application from the client at the end of each sprint. The length of the sprints also helps us get approval regarding development as we will be able to demonstrate a high quality application with each meeting with our client as a result of unit, integration, and user-acceptance testing.

Challenges & Solutions

openEHR Understanding

The inital challenge with openEHR was that it was a very specialised and complex concept/approach to understand. We did not know how openEHR worked and we could not initially comprehend its overarching design and architecture. Therefore, our attempt to solve this was to research into openEHR using various different methods such as watching tutorial videos, reading tutorials on the openEHR website and searching for existing openEHR applications on GitHub that resemble our application. This research throughout lasted for most of the project but eventually we were able to grasp a strong understanding of openEHR and how it worked with regards to its architecture design.


openEHR Implementation

Despite our success in understanding the openEHR approach, we still had to tackle the obstacle of implementing it. Although we knew how it worked, we had to attempt to implement it while avoiding complex measures. Initially, the only solution we had was designing the openEHR backend ourselves using the ADL (Architecture Description Language) Workbench, and Template & Archetype Editors. This process was estimated to take a long time and extra research to accomplish, and due to time constraints we had to look for other alternatives. As a result, we managed to solve this problem by getting into contact with openEHR expert Dr Ian McNicoll through our client Paul Fleming. Dr McNicoll was able to give us a formal introduction and tutorial on openEHR and EhrScape, an interface to the openEHR Clinical Data Repository. After he provided us with account details and a customised account for openEHR implementation on the EhrScape domain, we were able to get started on implementing the openEHR aspects of our project.

User Interface Design & User Experience

User Experience is concerned with the overall experience for a user using the app, with regards to ease of use and enjoyability. When designing our User Interface, we followed a predefined set of Design Principles as shown on the UI Design page. To summarise, these principles were; Simplicity, Simplicity, Visibility, Feedback, Tolerant and Consistency. Overall, we believe we have adhered to these principles as this is reflected by the positive feedback we have consitently received from our client throughout the project regarding the User Interface. Project Management

Project management usually involves the disciplines of intiating, planning, executing, controlling and closing of a project by a team in order to accomplish predefined goals.
I will evaluate our effectiveness in each of the five disciplines below.

Initiation - This involved our ability to identify the project needs and problems at the start of the project and I believe we managed to accomplish this very effectively. Initially, we performed intensive research into the field of Electronic Health Records (EHR) and Care Plans to get a strong understanding of the context of our project. In addition, we researched into the Market and Exisiting Solutions for the EHR industry to further our understanding of the industry we will be working within and obtain ideas for future development. Lastly, before we began we made sure to communicate efficiently with our client to gain as much knowledge about the background, context and inspirations of this project. This meant that we would be able to effectively identify the problems of the status quo and move onto the next stage of the process without obstacles.

Planning - This process involved the ability for the (Deputy) Team Leader to plan out all future steps in order to achieve the goals of the project. I believe we organised ourselves quite effectively as we maintained a clean and organised Google Drive folder structure where we stored all relevant documents for planning and delegation of tasks. In addition, we planned future work on Trello which happened to be an invaluable platform due to convenience and ease of use. Furthermore, we produced a Gantt Chart for both terms which helped us plan and schedule all our tasks around set deadlines. Lastly, we assigned team roles based on our individual strenghts and weaknesses so that we could maximise productivity and reduce downtime due to learning curves. The strengths and weaknesses of each team member also contributed when both Team Leaders delegated tasks. However, with regards to mistakes, our research/planning lacked on openEHR and as a result our progress was slowed down during the execution phase. An improvement for future projects would be to plan in more detail so that it is entirely understood what needs to be done during development.

Execution - This phase consisted of the ability to carry out tasks according to our project plan in order to achieve the deliverables of the project. We performed admirably on this section despite many drawbacks such as ineffective planning on openEHR and a demotivated team member. As a result, work was slow initially. However, due to frequent team meetings and consistent communication on platforms like WhatsApp, Facebook and Skype, we were able to overcome these obstacles and maximise productivity. For the majority of the execution phase, the team was able to work effectively together on App Development and Project Website Development to reach the project goals and deliverables. On the other hand again, there were some disadvantages maintaining efficiency and work ethic. Usually, development consisted of being periods of intensive work followed by breaks. This led to unnecessary downtime and loss of work efficiency.

Monitoring - This process involved the constant checking up of project factors like time and quality during the execution process. The quality of code produced was high as the Technical Lead ensured that Computer Science conventions were met to ensure cleanliness and structured code. In addition, the Deputy Team Leader ensured through that documentation produced either on the website or other reports was of high quality by proof reading everything before submission. On the other hand, regarding time management, we were able to monitor this effectively by frequently referring to the Gantt Charts we created to help schedule our tasks. As a result, we managed to stay on top of deadlines for the majority of tasks. However, in several circumstances, due to a demotivated team member some tasks were not completed on time and this led the entire team to extend deadlines to accommodate for this.

Closing - This phase involves making sure all goals have been met, approved and final deliverables are ready to be handed over to the client. We made sure that after all the features had been implemented we handed over the mobile app to the client for User Acceptance Testing and thus gathered their feedback and eventually their approval. In addition, we transferred our completed our project website including all documentation, User and Systems Manuals, and GitHub repository with all app code for future work to our client.
